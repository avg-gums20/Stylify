Sustainable Materials

Sustainable materials in fashion include organic cotton, hemp, Tencel, and recycled fabrics. These materials use fewer natural resources and reduce environmental impact during production. For instance, organic cotton requires significantly less water than conventional cotton, while Tencel is made from wood pulp in a closed-loop process

Water Consumption in Fashion

The fashion industry is a major consumer of water. From cotton production to dyeing processes, vast amounts of water are used. For example, it takes about 2,700 liters of water to produce one cotton t-shirt. Sustainable fashion aims to reduce water usage by using water-efficient technologies and materials like rain-fed cotton.

Microplastics & Synthetic Fabrics

Synthetic fabrics like polyester, nylon, and acrylic release microplastics into waterways when washed. These microplastics are too small to be filtered out and contribute to ocean pollution, harming marine life. Choosing natural fibers or washing synthetics less frequently helps reduce this pollution.

Fashion Waste & Recycling

Fast fashion has led to an increase in textile waste, with millions of tons of clothing ending up in landfills each year. Clothing recycling, upcycling, and creating circular fashion models—where garments are reused, repaired, and recycled—are key to reducing waste and minimizing the industry's impact.
